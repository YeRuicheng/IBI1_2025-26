This practical about stochastic SIR models is very interesting and practical. 
The basic SIR model is easy to implement, but understanding the probability calculation of infection takes some time. 
The vaccination part helps me learn the concept of herd immunity intuitively. 
The spatial SIR model is more challenging, especially dealing with 8-neighbor infection and boundary problems. 
I learned how to convert real epidemic problems into probabilistic models and use Python for simulation and visualization. 
The most important thing is that stochastic models can show different results each time, which is closer to the real world.
